Non-Degenerate Construction and the Kernel of Admissibility

Plainly: this paper asks what has to be in place before a sequence of steps can count as constructing a determinate object rather than merely changing descriptions.

Technically: it isolates the fixed-domain kernel K = {Adm, St, Ref, Irr}: admissibility, standing, reference, and irreversibility. The later archive inherits this as the governance layer for identity-bearing construction.

Technically: the AMetric boundary fixes a non-transmissive interface for admissibility. It blocks re-metricization, illicit scope transport, and claims of deeper pre-metric invariants beneath the admissibility boundary.
